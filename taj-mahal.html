1. TAJ MAHAL

The Taj Mahal is a stunning white marble building in Agra, India. It was built by Emperor Shah Jahan in the 17th century as a memorial for his beloved wife, Mumtaz Mahal, who passed away. The Taj Mahal is known for its beautiful, intricate designs and is a symbol of eternal love. The main part of the Taj Mahal is a large dome surrounded by four minarets. It sits in the middle of a vast garden, with a reflecting pool in front of it that mirrors the building's elegance. The walls are covered with delicate carvings and precious stones, making it one of the most visually impressive structures in the world. Today, the Taj Mahal attracts millions of visitors from around the globe who come to admire its beauty and learn about its history. It is not just a popular tourist spot but also a UNESCO World Heritage Site, recognized for its cultural significance and architectural brilliance.
